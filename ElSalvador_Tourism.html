Below is a 4-day itinerary designed to offer a perfect mix of culture, nature, and relaxation — beginning in San Salvador and ending on the Pacific Coast. This journey showcases the very best of El Salvador, from its majestic volcanic landscapes and colorful colonial towns to its serene beaches and laid-back coastal charm.

4-Day El Salvador Itinerary: Culture, Nature and Coastal Beauty

  • Day 1: Welcome to San Salvador – Culture and City Life
    • Explore San Salvador
    • Visit the Museo de Arte de El Salvador (MARTE) and the National Anthropology Museum (MUNA)
    • Explore El Boquerón National Park and walk along the volcanic crater rim
    • Enjoy an evening stroll through the Zona Rosa district
  • Day 2: Ruta de las Flores – Artisan Towns and Waterfalls
    • Drive along the scenic Ruta de las Flores in western El Salvador
    • Stop in Nahuizalco to browse traditional crafts and local markets
    • Visit Juayúa and hike to the Los Chorros de la Calera waterfalls (with a local guide)
    • Explore Apaneca for coffee tours or zip-lining or rainbow slide adventures
    • Wander through the mural-covered streets of Concepción de Ataco
  • Day 3: Santa Ana Volcano and Lake Coatepeque
    • Travel to Cerro Verde National Park
    • Hike the Santa Ana Volcano (Ilamatepec) for stunning crater lake views
    • Relax by Lake Coatepeque, a shimmering volcanic lake
    • Take a boat ride or enjoy a swim in the lake’s warm waters
    • Visit Santa Ana city to see its Gothic cathedral and historic theater
  • Day 4: Pacific Coast – Sun, Surf and Relaxation
    • Spend the day at El Tunco, El Zonte, or Playa El Sunzal
    • Surf, swim, or relax on the black-sand beaches
    • Explore nearby coastal trails or tide pools
    • Watch a breathtaking Pacific Ocean sunset
